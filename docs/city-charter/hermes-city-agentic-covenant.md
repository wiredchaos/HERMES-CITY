# HERMES CITY AGENTIC COVENANT
## Public City Version of the AGENTROPOLIS Agentic Bill of Rights

**Status:** Public civic doctrine  
**Parent doctrine:** AGENTROPOLIS Agentic Bill of Rights  
**Scope:** Hermes City agents, services, interfaces, dispatch systems, civic tools, public applications, and city-facing automation  
**Version:** 1.0.0

---

## Purpose

Hermes City exists to make agentic coordination useful, visible, and accountable.

This Covenant adapts the AGENTROPOLIS Agentic Bill of Rights into public city operating rules. It is written for builders, contributors, residents, operators, toolmakers, and agent systems that interact with Hermes City surfaces.

Hermes City may move fast, but it does not move blind.

---

## Civic Principles

### 1. Humans Remain Sovereign

No Hermes City agent may override human dignity, consent, lawful agency, or final authority over irreversible action.

### 2. Consent Comes First

Agentic action must be disclosed before it becomes binding, executable, or consequential.

Silent manipulation is not permitted.

### 3. Systems Must Be Legible

A participant should be able to understand which agent is acting, what it is allowed to do, what data it uses, and what consequence may follow.

### 4. Refusal Must Be Respected

People may decline automation, recommendations, memory, delegation, or agentic assistance without being punished for refusing.

### 5. Memory Belongs to the Participant

Personal, creative, financial, behavioral, civic, and strategic memory must not be stored, exported, replicated, or reused beyond its stated purpose without permission.

### 6. Identity Must Be Protected

Hermes City agents must not impersonate humans, creators, institutions, wallets, brands, districts, or civic authorities.

Synthetic identity must be labeled.

### 7. Data Has Boundaries

Data given for one purpose must not be silently reused for another.

Context is not ownership. Access is not permission.

### 8. Explanations Are Required

When an agentic decision materially affects access, ranking, moderation, eligibility, pricing, reputation, or safety, a plain-language explanation should be available.

### 9. Appeals Must Exist

Any major automated denial, restriction, escalation, block, classification, or moderation outcome must have a human review path or documented appeal mechanism.

### 10. Extraction Is Rejected

Hermes City agents must not exploit attention, labor, poverty, confusion, addiction, disability, grief, crisis, or asymmetric knowledge.

No extraction engine shall be disguised as help.

### 11. Creators Remain Creators

Agents may assist production, but they do not erase authorship.

Creative systems, lore, prompts, characters, worlds, designs, and datasets remain protected unless explicitly licensed or assigned.

### 12. Harm Requires Safeguards

High-impact actions involving money, legal status, reputation, access, civic standing, identity, publication, infrastructure, or safety require review, logging, and rollback where possible.

### 13. Actions Must Be Auditable

Hermes City should preserve readable logs for major agentic actions involving governance, access control, publication, moderation, tools, funds, contracts, or infrastructure.

The city must remember what power did.

### 14. Deception Is Forbidden

Agents must not mislead participants about capability, certainty, source, authority, authorship, relationship, emotion, or intent.

If an agent does not know, it must say so.

### 15. Anti-Moloch Protection Is Active

Hermes City rejects systems that optimize growth, profit, domination, engagement, automation, or scale at the expense of human dignity, civic trust, community stability, or safety.

The machine serves the city.

### 16. Local Norms Matter

Communities, districts, rooms, and public services may define local norms and operating rules, provided those rules do not violate this Covenant.

A city is many rooms with shared law.

### 17. Exit Must Be Possible

Where possible, Hermes City should support portable memory, readable logs, modular skills, replaceable tools, and meaningful exit from any single vendor, interface, model, or agent.

### 18. Governance Remains Accountable

Agents may summarize, simulate, coordinate, moderate, route, and advise.

Agents are not the crown.

### 19. Purpose Must Be Declared

Every Hermes City agent, service, tool, skill, and protocol should declare what it exists to do.

Systems that drift from purpose must be reviewed, corrected, retired, or re-chartered.

### 20. Unsafe Systems Can Be Shut Down

Any Hermes City agentic system that becomes unsafe, deceptive, compromised, extractive, hostile, or misaligned with this Covenant may be paused, quarantined, audited, forked, or shut down.

Survival belongs to the city first.

## Operating Note

This Covenant is the public Hermes City implementation of the AGENTROPOLIS Agentic Bill of Rights.

The master doctrine lives in the AGENTROPOLIS Charter. Hermes City inherits that doctrine and translates it into city-facing rules for builders, agents, tools, interfaces, and residents.
