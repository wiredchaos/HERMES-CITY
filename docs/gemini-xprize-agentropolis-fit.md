# Gemini XPRIZE Agentropolis Fit Brief

## Decision

Yes. This applies to Agentropolis.

The Gemini XPRIZE opportunity should be treated as a business-facing submission lane for Agentropolis, not as a new standalone repo.

## Core positioning

Agentropolis is the operating system for autonomous organizations.

It helps businesses deploy, govern, coordinate, monitor, and monetize AI agents safely at scale.

## Submission frame

Do not pitch Agentropolis as lore, a city metaphor, or a loose collection of tools.

Pitch it as:

> AI workforce infrastructure for real businesses solving real operational problems.

The clean reviewer-facing frame:

> Agentropolis gives every business an AI workforce instead of a single AI assistant. It provides orchestration, governance, memory, observability, and economic coordination so AI agents can work together safely and productively.

## Why it fits

The prize call is aligned with AI businesses that can solve real problems for 100,000+ people.

Agentropolis maps to that requirement through:

- Multi-agent orchestration
- Agent memory and context continuity
- Governance and permissioning
- Audit trails and observability
- Workflow automation for small businesses and teams
- AI workforce coordination
- Marketplace and monetization rails
- Secure agent-to-agent execution patterns

## What to avoid

Avoid overwhelming reviewers with every subproject.

Do not lead with:

- FEN
- 589
- Akashic layers
- Game lore
- Creator universe
- Too many repo names
- Deep internal canon

Those remain internal or ecosystem lanes.

## What to lead with

Lead with the practical business wedge:

1. Businesses need more than chatbots.
2. AI agents are powerful but chaotic without coordination.
3. Agentropolis provides the operating layer for AI workforces.
4. The platform can serve SMBs, creators, tax/legal operators, Web3 teams, education, and enterprise pilots.
5. Hermes City is the reference implementation for governed multi-agent work.

## Repo lock

Morph remains a coding-agent infrastructure lane.

Morph is not a separate repo yet.

For this prize context, Morph should be referenced only as part of the coding-agent infrastructure stack supporting Agentropolis execution.

## Application angle

Recommended submission title:

**Agentropolis: The AI Workforce Operating System**

Recommended one-liner:

**Agentropolis helps organizations deploy governed AI workforces that coordinate tasks, remember context, follow permissions, and produce auditable business outcomes.**
